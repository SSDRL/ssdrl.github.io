Open Vocabulary Mobile Manipulation


Full-day workshop at CoRL 2023

Nov 6-9 2023, Pacific Time (PT), Atlanta (USA)

Introduction

As roboticists, we envision a future where robots are seamlessly integrated into everyday life, aiding humans in daily tasks such as tidying a room or brewing a cup of tea. Such robots must not only navigate unfamiliar environments and manipulate unseen objects, but also communicate with everyday users through natural language. In this workshop, we delve into the concept of open-vocabulary mobile manipulation (OVMM), exploring how we can instruct robots using open-ended natural language and leverage large foundational models to enhance the semantic understanding of these robots. A critical element of our discussion will also be the recent advancements in integrating navigation and manipulation. Our ultimate goal is to reveal new opportunities for generalization, driven by open-vocabulary language instructions and physically adept robotic hardware and algorithms.

In this workshop, our primary focus will be on:
  • Task specification with open vocabulary language instructions
  • Representation learning
  • Utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs)
  • Developing open-vocabulary perception algorithms
  • Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
  • General purpose manipulation
  • Semantic scene understanding
  • Benchmarks and Datasets
  • Mobile manipulation hardwares
