Scalable synthetic data generation for robot learning


Full-day hybrid workshop

Recent advances in large language and vision-language models have demonstrated the power of scale—both in data and model capacity—for achieving generalization and robustness. Similar trends are emerging in robotics, where large and diverse datasets are enabling the development of more capable and transferable policies. However, unlike NLP or vision, large-scale data collection in robotics—especially through human teleoperation—remains prohibitively time-consuming and expensive. This workshop will explore emerging strategies to overcome this bottleneck through scalable synthetic data generation. These strategies span simulation-based methods (e.g., motion planning, trajectory optimization, and reinforcement learning), generative models (e.g., from human video demonstrations or across robot morphologies), and real2sim2real pipelines that leverage high-fidelity asset and scene reconstruction to bridge the sim-to-real gap. Our goal is to bring together researchers working across these directions to discuss the frontiers of robot data generation at scale.

In this workshop, we plan to discuss:
  • What are the strengths and limitations of current approaches to synthetic data generation?
  • How can we improve the fidelity and diversity of synthetic data—particularly from simulators—to better support real-world policy learning?
  • What are the trade-offs between domain realism and dataset scale, and how can we mitigate the sim-to-real gap?
  • BHow can generative models be leveraged to synthesize high-quality robot data?
  • What role can multi-embodiment datasets (e.g., RT-X, Droid) play in scaling data and enabling generalist agents?
  • How should we benchmark the quality and utility of synthetic data?

FAQ

Are you going to record the talks and post them later on YouTube?
We’re going to post the talks of speakers who permit us to on YouTube. But we will NOT post the recordings of the panel discussion, the debate, or the open discussion at the end.
Can I present remotely if my paper is accepted as a poster or a spotlight talk?
We will play a pre-recording of your spotlight talk and we will strongly encourage you to find friends to help present the poster in person.
